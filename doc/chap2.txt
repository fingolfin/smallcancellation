  [1X2.1 [33X[0;0YIntroduction[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YA  standard  reference for Small Cancellation Theory is Lyndon-Schupp [LS01,
  Chapter V]. We review here some definitions and results.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YA  subset [22XR[122X of a free group [22XF[122X is called [13Xsymmetrized[113X if all elements of [22XR[122X are
  cyclically  reduced and for each [22Xr[122X in [22XR[122X all cyclically reduced conjugates of
  [22Xr[122X  and  [22Xr^-1[122X  are  also  in  [22XR[122X.  If the set [22XR[122X is not symmetrized we may work
  instead  with  the  [13Xsymmetrization[113X  [22XR^*[122X  of  [22XR[122X (the smallest symmetrized set

  [33X[0;0YA [13Xpiece[113X of [22XR[122X is a word that is a common prefix of two different words in the
  symmetrized set [22XR^*[122X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe  say  that  a  presentation  [22XP=‚ü®  X ‚à£ R‚ü©[122X [13Xsatisfies the small cancellation
  condition [22XC(p)[122X[113X if no relator [22Xr[122X in [22XR^*[122X is a product of fewer than [22Xp[122X pieces.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe  say that [22XP[122X [13Xsatisfies the small cancellation condition [22XC'(Œª)[122X[113X if for all [22Xr[122X
  in [22XR^*[122X, if [22Xr=bc[122X and [22Xb[122X is a piece then [22X|b| < Œª |r|[122X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWe  say  that [22XP[122X [13Xsatisfies the small cancellation condition [22XT(q)[122X[113X if for all [22Xh[122X
  such that [22X3‚â§ h < q[122X and for all elements [22Xr_1,..., r_h[122X in [22XR^*[122X, if no succesive
  elements  [22Xr_i,r_i+1[122X  is  an  inverse pair, then at least one of the products
  [22Xr_1r_2,...  r_h-1r_h, r_hr_1[122X is reduced without cancellation. Condition [22XT(q)[122X
  may be rephrased in terms of the Whitehead graph of the presentation [22XP[122X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIf  a  group  [22XG=‚ü® X ‚à£ R‚ü©[122X satisfies [22XC'(1/6)[122X then Dehn's algorithm (see [LS01,
  Chapter V, Section 4]) solves the word problem for [22XG[122X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIf  a  group  [22XG=‚ü® X ‚à£ R‚ü©[122X satisfies [22XC(6)[122X or [22XC(4)‚ÄìT(4)[122X or [22XC(3)‚ÄìT(6)[122X then [22XG[122X has
  solvable  word problem and solvable conjugacy problem (see [LS01, Chapter V,

  [33X[0;0YIf  a presentation [22XP=‚ü® X ‚à£ R‚ü©[122X satisfies [22XC(6)[122X and no relator of [22XP[122X is a proper
